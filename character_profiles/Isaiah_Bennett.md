# Isaiah Bennett

## Basic Info
- **Role:** Whistleblower (deceased before Book 2 begins)
- **Status:** Environmental engineer/geologist killed by police
- **Profession:** Environmental engineer specializing in mining operations and groundwater analysis
- **Identity:** Black man, likely mid-30s to early 40s
- **Death:** Killed by police at peaceful protest

## Professional Background
- Environmental engineer or geologist
- Consulted on early phase of CADENS Southwest fusion facility project
- Expert in: Mining operations, environmental impact assessment, groundwater contamination, safety protocols
- Worked with Titan Strategic (unknowingly part of Kain's shell company network)
- Professional reputation: Ethical, thorough, safety-focused

## What He Discovered
**Fusion Project Violations:**
- Safety violations in construction timeline (18-24 months = dangerously rushed)
- Environmental crimes: Groundwater contamination, illegal dumping
- Mining exploitation: Extraction economy destroying surrounding communities
- Environmental racism: Sacrifice zones targeting marginalized populations
- Money laundering through shell companies
- Connection between Titan Strategic and Kain's campaign

**Timeline:**
- Hired during early phase of project (preliminary environmental assessments)
- Discovered violations as construction accelerated
- Attempted to report through proper channels (likely blocked)
- Prepared to go public before Kain's campaign announcement
- Killed by police at peaceful protest approximately 6-8 months before Book 2 begins

## Social Media Smear Campaign

**Method of Suppression:**
Kain's team weaponized social media against Isaiah:
- Deepfake videos showing fabricated misconduct
- Bot army amplifying fake scandals
- Coordinated harassment campaign
- Character assassination (professional credibility destroyed)
- Isolation from support networks
- Discredited whistleblower before he could speak publicly

**Why This Method:**
- Discredits whistleblower before they can speak
- If information leaks, source already delegitimized
- Appears organic rather than coordinated hit
- Multiple vectors of attack (harder to defend against)
- Psychological warfare more effective than physical threats initially

**When Physical Elimination Occurred:**
When Isaiah persisted despite smear campaign, he was killed by police at a peaceful protest. Chief Whitford's department involved in the killing.

## Police Killing

## Writing Notes

**When Revealing Isaiah's Story:**
- Let information emerge gradually (mystery structure)
- Show impact on Tess as she learns about father's role
- Honor his memory (not just plot device)
- Community grief should feel real
- Environmental justice context matters
- Don't make his death "worth it" - tragedy remains tragedy

**Avoid:**
- Magical Negro trope (he's not here to teach white characters lessons)
- Fridging (death purely for character development)
- Simplifying his character to "victim" only
- Making his death feel inevitable or justified

**Aim For:**
- Fully realized person with community, family, professional legacy
- Death feels like genuine loss (not just inciting incident)
- His work and warnings matter beyond his death
- Police violence connected to systemic corruption
- Both victim AND agent of his own story (made choices, took risks knowingly)
