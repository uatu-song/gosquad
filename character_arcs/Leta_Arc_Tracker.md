# Leta Owolowo - Character Arc Tracker

**Purpose:** Track Leta's emotional state, knowledge, relationships, and capabilities at each major story point

**Note:** Leta dies in Book 2. Her death is a major catalyst for Korede's radicalization and Tess's arc.

---

## Character Foundation

### Basic Info
- **Full Name:** Leta Owolowo
- **Identity:** Black trans woman (she/her pronouns)
- **Role:** Activist, community organizer (NOT Go Squad member - civilian with personal stakes)
- **Relationships:**
  - Tess Whitford (girlfriend)
  - Korede Owolowo (younger brother)
- **Location:** Caledonia, on front lines of protests

### Personality & Values
- Passionate about justice, community organizing
- Protective of younger brother Korede
- Trans woman of color = compounded danger in police crackdowns
- Full character: activist, organizer, person Tess loves (not defined solely by transness)

### Skills
- Community organizing
- Protest coordination
- Direct action planning
- Building mutual aid networks

**The Event:** Isaiah Bennett memorial protest turns violent.

**Leta's Role:**
- At protest as organizer
- Korede also present (she's watching him)
- Witnesses violence, sees Ahdia save people

**State After:**
- **Emotional:** Grief for Isaiah. Determination.
- **Knowledge:** Violence is escalating
- **Relationships:** Tess protective, split between Go Squad and Leta

---

### Chapters 2-5: Front Line Work (Months 1-2)

**Leta's Activities:**
- Protest organizing
- Community resistance building
- Mutual aid networks
- Coordinating with Victor's networks

**Relationship Dynamics:**
- Tests Go Squad: "Whose side are you on?"
- Moral compass challenging team's institutional ties
- Protective of Korede (worried about his involvement)

**State:** Established activist, partner, sister.

---

### Chapter 6: Harassment Begins (Month 3)

**THE TARGETING:**
- Identified as "Tess's girlfriend" through social media
- Coordinated online attacks begin:
  - Transphobic harassment (misgendering, deadnaming)
  - Death threats
  - Doxxing attempts
  - Racist and sexist slurs compounded
- Eidolon amplifies harassers' hatred

**State After:**
- **Emotional:** Stressed but continuing
- **Relationships:** Tess protective, relationship strained by pressure

---

### Chapters 7-10: Harassment Escalates (Months 3-5)

**Escalation:**
- Threats become more specific
- Doxxing successful—home address leaked
- Deepfakes circulate
- Bot networks coordinate attacks

**Leta's Response:**
- Sets boundaries (won't do emotional labor for white allies)
- Develops **fear resistance practice** (justified vs distorted fear)
- Continues organizing despite threats
- Direct with Leah: "When you say nothing, people assume the whole team agrees with the attacks. Your silence hurts."

**The Practice (60-70% effective):**
- Distinguishing justified fear from Eidolon-amplified distortion
- Teaches this to others through Victor's networks
- Counters Eidolon's amplification

---

### Chapter 11: Harassment Severe (Month 5, Late)

**Credible Danger:**
- Death threats credible and specific
- Tess desperate to protect her
- Safety protocols implemented
- Organizing work continues despite risk

**State After:**
- **Emotional:** Fear (justified) but continuing
- **Knowledge:** Real threat to her life exists
- **Relationships:** Tess fiercely protective

---

### Chapters 12-17: Resilience (Months 6-8)

**Leta's Continued Work:**
- Fear resistance practice spreads through Victor's networks
- Teaching others to distinguish justified vs distorted fear
- Organizing continues

**Parallel Events:**
- TRIOMF discovered
- Evidence leaks
- Eidolon reframes

**State:** Resilient despite targeting. Teaching others.

---

### Chapters 18-20: Living Under Threat (Months 9-10)

**Leta's State:**
- Chronic harassment continues
- Fear resistance practice keeping her functional
- Knows she's in real danger
- Continues anyway

---

### Chapter 21: Death (Month 11)

**THE EVENT:** Citywide manhunt. Eidolon turns civilians into weapons. Thousands hunting Go Squad.

**What Happens:**
- Leta protecting Korede during chaos
- **Officer Webb kills her**
- Same cop who killed Isaiah Bennett
- Tess witnesses the murder
- Korede witnesses the murder

**Immediate Aftermath:**
- Tess shattered—loses girlfriend
- Korede traumatized—loses sister, sees her killer
- In chaos, Tess reveals Go Squad identity to Korede
- Webb walks away (will be "exonerated" again)

**State:** Dead.

---

### Book 2 Arc Summary

**The Martyrdom Arc:**
- Activist partner → Targeted for visibility → Developed resistance practice → Taught others → Killed by system

**What She Contributed:**
- Fear resistance practice (60-70% effective against Eidolon)
- Moral compass for Go Squad
- Challenged white moderate comfort (Leah)
- Protected Korede as long as she could

**How She Died:**
- Killed by Officer Webb (same cop who killed Isaiah)
- Protecting Korede during manhunt
- Witnessed by Tess and Korede
- Webb will be exonerated again

**What Her Death Caused:**
- Tess: Vengeance arc (brutalizes Webb Ch 23)
- Korede: Radicalization (observer → fighter)
- Leah: Guilt ("too late for Leta")
- Team: Personal loss, not just abstract stakes
