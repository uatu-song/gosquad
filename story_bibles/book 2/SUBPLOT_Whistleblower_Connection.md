**Dramatic Irony:**

## Precious Metals Extraction

### Why Fusion Needs Rare Earths

**Technical Requirements:**
- Magnetic containment systems require massive rare earth magnets
- Neodymium, dysprosium, samarium for superconducting coils
- Cooling systems need specialized materials
- Tons of rare earths for single reactor
- Rushed timeline = increased material demands

**Extraction Locations:**
- Domestic mining operations (Western US, Native American land?)
- International sourcing (exploitation of Global South)
- Environmental devastation:
  - Open pit mining
  - Toxic waste runoff
  - Groundwater contamination
  - Habitat destruction
  - Air pollution from processing
- Community exploitation:
  - Land seizures
  - Health impacts ignored
  - Economic coercion
  - Protests suppressed

**Titan Strategic Connection:**
- Same shell company funding Tank Kain AND fusion project
- Also owns/contracts mining operations
- Vertical integration: Mine materials, build reactor, suppress opposition
- Financial trail connects riots, mining, fusion, everything

**Environmental Irony Deepened:**
- "Clean energy" built on extractive economy
- Destroying ecosystems to mine materials for "environmental solution"
- Communities poisoned to build reactor that will "save planet"
- Isaiah discovered: Environmental cost outweighs benefit in this implementation
- Both/and: Fusion COULD be clean, this extraction ISN'T

## Writing Notes

### Mystery Structure

**Month 1-5: Separate Investigations**
- Each investigator thinks their angle is unique
- Ben: Fusion safety
- Tess: Isaiah's killing by police (father's involvement)
- Leta: Mining exploitation
- Show clues but don't connect them yet
- Reader might notice patterns before characters do

**Month 6-7: Convergence**
- Characters compare notes, realize connections
- "Wait, you're investigating mining too?"
- "The bot patterns match across campaigns?"
- "Titan Strategic keeps appearing..."
- Revelation: Everything connects to fusion

**Month 8: Public Exposure**
- Comprehensive case includes all threads
- Too complex for public to follow
- Perfect example of both/and: Evidence valid AND ineffective

### Isaiah as Character (Appears in Flashbacks/Discoveries)

**Not Just Name:**
- Give him life through others' memories
- Environmental engineer passionate about justice
- Family shown grieving, never believed official story
- Colleagues remember him as principled
- Community he tried to protect honors him
- He's person, not plot device

**Tess's Connection:**
- She attends protests about his killing (Chapter 11)
- Learns about him gradually (investigation discoveries through father's files)
- Eventually meets his family? (devastating, necessary)
- Her arc: From "police say justified" to "father orchestrated assassination"

### Balancing Complexity

**Don't Overwhelm Reader:**
- Introduce connections gradually
- Visual aids in narrative? (Ben's evidence boards, photos)
- Character explanations to other characters (natural exposition)
- Trust reader to track multiple threads
- Payoff when connections click

**Keep Human Scale:**
- Big conspiracy, but focus on people
- Isaiah's family grieving
- Tess's ethical conflict with father
- Mining community members with names, faces, stories
- Not just data points, actual people
