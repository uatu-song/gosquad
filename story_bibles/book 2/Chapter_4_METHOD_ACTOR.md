## SCENE 1: Bourn/Patterson (Institutional Vulnerability)

**Touchpoint A:** SecDef Patterson contacts Bourn
**Touchpoint B:** Bourn realizes CADENS could be weaponized by the wrong President

**Setting:** CADENS headquarters, classified level. Bourn alone in secure hologram room. SecDef Patterson incoming call.

**POV:** Bourn (institutional parallel to Ahdia's personal cage)

---

[NARRATOR]: The hologram room was designed for secrets. Soundproofed walls, signal encryption that would take a quantum computer decades to crack, no windows, no witnesses. Harriet Bourn had held conversations in this room that would have ended careers, toppled governments, prevented wars.

Today she stood alone, waiting for a call she'd been dreading for weeks.

The projector hummed. Light coalesced into the shape of a man in a Pentagon office—SecDef Patterson, his face arranged in the careful neutrality of someone about to threaten you politely.

[AS PATTERSON, no pleasantries]:
"Director Bourn."

[AS BOURN, matching his tone]:
"Mr. Secretary. To what do I owe the pleasure?"

[AS PATTERSON]:
"Cut the civility. We both know why I'm calling."

[NARRATOR]: Bourn waited. She'd learned decades ago that the person who speaks first in these conversations loses.

[AS PATTERSON]:
"Geneva Windrow. You had CADENS assets investigating her assassination."

[AS BOURN]:
"Consultation at the request of local law enforcement—"

[AS PATTERSON, cutting her off]:
"Consultation. With your enhanced assets. Off the books."

[NARRATOR]: Bourn's face stayed neutral. So Patterson had been monitoring CADENS activity. That wasn't surprising. What was surprising was that he was admitting it.

[AS PATTERSON]:
"CADENS operates with near-total autonomy. Defense doesn't get briefed. Intelligence doesn't get briefed. You answer to one person—the President—and even that's more courtesy than requirement."

[AS BOURN]:
"Our charter is Presidential. That's been the arrangement for forty years."

[AS PATTERSON]:
"Your charter is a blank check written by an administration that's about to change."

[NARRATOR]: There it was. The threat, finally visible.

[AS BOURN, careful]:
"CADENS answers to whoever holds the office. That hasn't changed."

[AS PATTERSON]:
"Then you should be very interested in who that might be next year."

[NARRATOR]: Bourn felt the calculation happening—Patterson backing Kain, or at least hedging his bets. The polls had Kain up eight points since Geneva's death. The smart money was already shifting.

[AS PATTERSON]:
"When oversight finally comes—and it will come—CADENS will answer to more than just one person."

[AS BOURN]:
"Oversight. Or control?"

[AS PATTERSON]:
"Is there a difference?"

[AS BOURN]:
"CADENS was designed to operate independently for a reason—"

[AS PATTERSON]:
"To avoid accountability. Yes, I've read the documentation."

[AS BOURN]:
"To avoid political accountability. Cataclysm-derived threats don't wait for committee approval."

[AS PATTERSON]:
"And who decides what qualifies as 'cataclysm-derived'? You? Personally?"

[NARRATOR]: Bourn couldn't argue that point. She did have unilateral authority on threat classification. She'd designed it that way, back when she trusted herself completely.

[AS PATTERSON]:
"One person with access to cosmic-level weapons, operating in secret, answering to no oversight. Tell me, Director—how is that different from dictatorship?"

[AS BOURN, sharp]:
"Because I can be fired by the President. Tomorrow. With one signature."

[AS PATTERSON]:
"And if the President doesn't fire you? Then what? Who stops you?"

[NARRATOR]: Silence. The question hung in the recycled air.

[AS PATTERSON]:
"Do you know how many defense initiatives get killed in committee? Budget fights? Authorization deadlocks? Meanwhile, CADENS gets blank checks, black sites, and carte blanche."

[AS BOURN]:
"Because we deal with threats you can't classify, understand, or contain through conventional means."

[AS PATTERSON]:
"Or because you've convinced every administration you're indispensable."

[NARRATOR]: Bourn felt something shift in her chest. Not guilt, exactly. Recognition. Patterson wasn't wrong about everything.

[AS PATTERSON]:
"I'm recommending CADENS reform to Kain's transition team. Oversight. Accountability. Transparency where possible."

[AS BOURN]:
"You'll compromise operational security—"

[AS PATTERSON]:
"I'll compromise your operational autonomy. There's a difference."

[AS BOURN]:
"Not when threats move faster than oversight committees."

[AS PATTERSON]:
"Then maybe CADENS needs to move slower. More carefully. More accountably."

[AS BOURN]:
"Or maybe Defense needs to accept that some threats require trust."

[AS PATTERSON, cold]:
"Trust is earned, Director. And you haven't earned mine."

[NARRATOR]: The hologram flickered and died. Patterson disappeared, leaving Bourn alone in a room designed for secrets.

She stood in the dark for a long moment.

Is he wrong?

CADENS did operate with no oversight except Presidential. She did have unilateral authority. And if Kain became President—a man who'd orchestrated a massacre and blamed the victims—he would control everything she'd built.

The system she designed to prevent catastrophe could be weaponized by whoever controlled the Presidency.

She'd made CADENS powerful to protect America. What if that power was turned against it?

[NARRATOR]: Bourn pulled up a display—CADENS operations, enhanced individuals program, translocation tech, cataclysm research. All of it accessible to the President. All of it could be used for oppression, surveillance, control.

She'd designed it for defense. Kain could use it for offense.

And she couldn't refuse Presidential orders without violating CADENS charter. Without becoming exactly what Patterson accused her of being—an unaccountable dictator.

The cage became visible. Different from Ahdia's. Same bars.

## SCENE 2: Victor Patrol (Citizens Turn)

**Touchpoint A:** Victor on routine patrol, normal night
**Touchpoint B:** Victor nearly overwhelmed by civilians, barely escapes

**Setting:** Downtown Caledonia. Evening patrol. Victor checking in on a disturbance.

**POV:** Victor

---

[NARRATOR]: Victor had been doing this work for fifteen years. Community organizing, mutual aid, showing up when showing up mattered. He'd faced cops, fascists, corporate security. He'd buried a wife and kept going.

Tonight felt different. Wrong in a way he couldn't name.

The streets were quieter than usual for a Thursday. Shops closing early. People walking faster, heads down. A woman crossed the street to avoid passing him on the sidewalk.

He was wearing civilian clothes—jeans, hoodie, nothing that said "vigilante." Just a Black man walking through his own neighborhood.

That used to be enough.

[AS VICTOR, internal]:
Something's off. The air feels thick. Heavy. Like before a storm, except the sky is clear.

[NARRATOR]: His earpiece crackled.

[AS RUTH, over comms]:
"Victor, you're near Fifth and Morrison. We're getting reports of a disturbance—sounds like a mugging in progress."

[AS VICTOR]:
"On it. Two blocks out."

[NARRATOR]: He picked up his pace. This was the work. Someone needed help. You showed up.

The alley behind the bodega was dark, lit only by a flickering security light. Victor saw the shapes before he heard the voices—two figures, one pressed against the wall, one looming.

[AS VICTOR, stepping into the alley]:
"Hey. Everything okay here?"

[NARRATOR]: The looming figure turned. Young guy, maybe twenty, face twisted with something that looked like fear pretending to be anger.

[AS YOUNG MAN]:
"Mind your business."

[AS VICTOR, calm]:
"I'm making it my business. You okay back there?"

[NARRATOR]: The person against the wall—a woman, older, clutching her purse—nodded frantically.

[AS VICTOR]:
"Why don't you step back and let her go."

[NARRATOR]: The young man's hand twitched toward his pocket. Victor tracked the movement—could be a weapon, could be nothing. He'd de-escalated a hundred situations like this.

[AS YOUNG MAN, voice rising]:
"Who the hell are you? Some kind of hero?"

[AS VICTOR]:
"Just someone who lives here. Same as you."

[NARRATOR]: Something shifted in the young man's eyes. The fear-anger flickered, and for a second Victor thought he'd gotten through. Then the woman screamed.

Not at the young man. At Victor.

[AS WOMAN, pointing]:
"That's one of them! The vigilantes! He's one of them!"

[NARRATOR]: Victor's blood went cold.

[AS VICTOR]:
"Ma'am, I'm trying to help you—"

[AS WOMAN]:
"Help? You people are the problem! You brought this on us! The riots, the violence—"

[NARRATOR]: The young man backed away, forgotten. But now there were other sounds—footsteps, voices, people emerging from doorways. The woman was still screaming.

[AS VOICE FROM CROWD]:
"Is that Crimson Sable?"

[AS ANOTHER VOICE]:
"The vigilante? Get him!"

[NARRATOR]: Victor's training kicked in. Count the threats. Find the exits. Don't engage civilians.

Except the civilians were engaging him.

A man grabbed his arm. Victor twisted free—gently, no force, just redirection. But the man stumbled, and someone else shouted "He's attacking us!"

More hands. More bodies. The crowd was maybe fifteen people now, faces he might have recognized last week. Neighbors. Shop owners. Parents from the community center.

All of them looking at him like he was the enemy.

[AS VICTOR, internal]:
This isn't right. These people know me. I've helped them. What's happening?

[NARRATOR]: Someone threw a punch. Victor ducked, but another fist caught his shoulder. He could fight back—his training was better than theirs—but these weren't enemies. These were scared people, and hitting them would make everything worse.

[AS VICTOR, into comms]:
"Ruth, I need extraction. Fifth and Morrison, alley behind the bodega. Civilians have turned—it's like they're not themselves."

[AS RUTH, alarmed]:
"Turned? What do you mean turned?"

[AS VICTOR]:
"I mean they're trying to kill me and I don't think they know why."

[NARRATOR]: A bottle shattered against the wall near his head. The crowd was growing—twenty people now, maybe more. Victor backed toward the alley's dead end, which was exactly the wrong direction, but the street was blocked by bodies.

[AS VICTOR, internal]:
Fifteen years. Fifteen years of this work. And they're looking at me like I'm a monster.

[NARRATOR]: He saw the knife a second before it would have opened his throat. Grabbed the wrist, redirected, watched the blade clatter away. The attacker's face was blank with terror—not anger, Victor realized. Fear. They were all afraid. Desperately, irrationally afraid.

Of him.

[AS VICTOR]:
"Something's amplifying their fear. This isn't natural."

[AS RUTH]:
"Leah's en route. Two minutes."

[AS VICTOR]:
"I might not have two minutes."

[NARRATOR]: He made a decision. The fire escape to his left was rusted but climbable. He'd have to go through the crowd to reach it—and they'd have to choose not to follow.

Victor ran.

Three steps, four, feeling hands grab at his jacket. He jumped, caught the ladder, hauled himself up as fingers scraped at his ankles. The rust bit into his palms. He climbed.

Below, the crowd milled, confused. Whatever had gripped them was fading—he could see it in their faces, the fear flickering to bewilderment. They looked at each other, at their own hands, at the blood on Victor's jacket.

[AS VOICE FROM BELOW]:
"What... what happened?"

[NARRATOR]: Victor reached the rooftop. Sat down hard. His hands were shaking.

Fifteen years of building trust. One night of whatever that was.

And he hadn't even done anything wrong.

[AS VICTOR, into comms]:
"I'm clear. Rooftop on Morrison. But Ruth—we need to talk about what just happened. Something turned those people. Something made them afraid of me."

[AS RUTH]:
"Come in. We'll debrief."

[NARRATOR]: Victor looked down at the street. The crowd was dispersing, people wandering away like sleepwalkers waking up. None of them would remember why they'd attacked him. None of them would understand.

But he would remember their faces. Every single one.

## SCENE 3: Ruth/Bourn First Real Meeting

**Touchpoint A:** Bourn requests meeting with Ruth
**Touchpoint B:** Seeds planted for Ruth's institutional seduction

**Setting:** CADENS auxiliary facility. Neutral ground. Late evening.

**POV:** Ruth

---

[NARRATOR]: Ruth had been to CADENS headquarters exactly once—the day they'd recruited Firas, back when everything was simpler. This wasn't headquarters. This was a satellite facility, a converted warehouse on the edge of the industrial district. Neutral ground, Bourn had called it.

Ruth didn't believe in neutral ground. Every space belonged to someone.

The door was guarded by a single agent who checked her credentials without speaking. Inside, the warehouse had been converted into something that looked almost cozy—couches, a coffee station, soft lighting. It looked like someone's idea of what a spy would want for a private meeting.

Director Bourn was already there, standing by the coffee station like she'd been waiting. She was older than Ruth expected from her file—late fifties, silver hair, the kind of stillness that came from decades of power.

[AS BOURN, warm]:
"Dr. Carter. Thank you for coming."

[AS RUTH, guarded]:
"Your message said it was urgent."

[AS BOURN]:
"Please, sit. Coffee? It's better than it looks."

[NARRATOR]: Ruth sat. Didn't take the coffee. Watched Bourn pour herself a cup and settle into the chair opposite.

[AS BOURN]:
"I'll get straight to the point. I've been following your team's work. Impressive, given your resources."

[AS RUTH]:
"We manage."

[AS BOURN]:
"You do more than manage. You've built something real. A team that trusts each other, that shows up, that does the work. That's rare."

[NARRATOR]: Ruth waited. Compliments from people like Bourn always had a second half.

[AS BOURN]:
"I also know you're carrying a load that would break most people. Team leader. Medical officer. Primary treatment provider for your temporalist. Grief counselor, apparently, based on what I've observed."

[AS RUTH, sharper]:
"You've been observing us?"

[AS BOURN]:
"CADENS monitors enhanced individuals. It's not surveillance—it's protection. For you as much as anyone."

[AS RUTH]:
"That's a distinction I'm not sure I buy."

[AS BOURN, small smile]:
"Good. You shouldn't buy it automatically. Question everything, Dr. Carter. That's what makes you valuable."

[NARRATOR]: Ruth felt the manipulation happening—the flattery, the acknowledgment of her suspicion, the subtle framing of CADENS as ally rather than threat. She'd seen doctors do this with families. Build trust before delivering bad news.

[AS RUTH]:
"What do you want, Director?"

[AS BOURN]:
"I want to help. And I think you could use help."

[AS RUTH]:
"Help with what?"

[AS BOURN]:
"Everything. Resources. Intel. Institutional backing. Your team is doing important work with no support, no budget, no protection. I can offer all three."

[AS RUTH]:
"In exchange for?"

[AS BOURN]:
"Coordination. Not control—I know the difference, and I suspect you do too. But coordination. Sharing information. Working together instead of at cross purposes."

[NARRATOR]: Ruth's instincts were screaming warnings, but something else was happening too. A tired part of her, the part that had been holding everything together for months, heard "help" and wanted to believe it.

[AS RUTH]:
"Why me? Why not approach the whole team?"

[AS BOURN]:
"Because you're the leader. And because..." She paused. Set down her coffee. "Because I see something in you that I recognize. Someone who takes responsibility. Who can't stop taking responsibility, even when the weight is crushing."

[NARRATOR]: That landed. Ruth felt it in her chest.

[AS BOURN]:
"I've been that person for forty years. Leading CADENS, protecting people from threats they can't imagine. And I've learned something important: you can't do it alone. Not forever. The weight breaks you eventually, unless you let other people help carry it."

[AS RUTH]:
"Is this a recruitment pitch?"

[AS BOURN]:
"This is an offer. A partnership. You keep your independence, your team, your methods. But you have CADENS behind you when you need it. Resources, intel, backup. No strings—or at least, strings you can see and cut if you don't like them."

[NARRATOR]: Ruth wanted to say no. Wanted to walk out, maintain the clean separation between her team and the shadow agencies that had taken Firas.

But she was so tired. And Bourn was right about the weight.

[AS RUTH]:
"I'll think about it."

[AS BOURN, nodding]:
"That's all I ask. Here's my direct line. No intermediaries. Call me when you're ready to talk more."

[NARRATOR]: She handed Ruth a card—plain, just a phone number. No name, no title.

Ruth took it. Put it in her pocket.

She told herself she was keeping her options open. She told herself this didn't change anything.

But walking out of the warehouse, she felt something she hadn't felt in months: the faint, dangerous sensation of not being alone.
