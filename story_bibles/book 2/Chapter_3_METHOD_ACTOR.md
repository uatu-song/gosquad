## SCENE 1: Team Debrief

**Touchpoint A:** Team gathers morning after assassination
**Touchpoint B:** Ahdia has heard what the team needs to continue investigating

**Setting:** Penthouse. Next morning. Team gathered. Ahdia on couch, barely present.

---

[NARRATOR]: The penthouse felt different in daylight. Smaller, somehow. Ahdia had watched from this same couch as Geneva Windrow's head exploded on live television, and now here she was, eighteen hours later, watching Ruth pace in front of the windows like a general without a map.

The team had arranged themselves around the living room—Ben by the kitchen counter, Tess cross-legged on the floor with her laptop, Victor and Leah sharing the loveseat. Nobody had slept. Ahdia could tell by the way they held their coffee cups like weapons.

[AS RUTH, professional but strained]:
"Alright. What do we know?"

[NARRATOR]: Ruth's voice was the same one she used in triage. Calm, clipped, already moving to the next task. Ahdia recognized it from the riot. From the warehouse. From every moment when falling apart wasn't an option.

[AS BEN, military precision]:
"Sniper nest on the parking structure. Roof access, standard maintenance hatch—no forced entry. Someone had the codes or the skills. I found a dust circle on the gravel. Tripod imprint. Spent brass, 7.62 NATO."

[AS TESS, processing through facts]:
"Long range."

[AS BEN]:
"Eight hundred meters, moving target, headshot. Expert level. But that's not the interesting part."

[NARRATOR]: Ben paused. Ahdia watched him weighing what to say next.

[AS BEN]:
"My ears were ringing when I got there. Ozone smell. Like after a lightning strike."

[AS RUTH, connecting dots]:
"Translocation signature."

[AS BEN]:
"That's my guess. The shooter was there, took the shot, and then... gone. No egress route, no witnesses, no trace except the brass and the smell. But I can't trace it. I don't have access to—"

[AS RUTH, cutting him off]:
"CADENS tech."

[AS BEN]:
"Right. They track translocation signatures globally. If someone used their tech—or something like it—they'd have logs. Authorization records. But we're not exactly on the Christmas card list."

[NARRATOR]: Tess looked up from her laptop.

[AS TESS, found something]:
"I found something too. The bot network that pushed the 'radical elements' narrative? The one that had Kain's talking points ready before the ambulance arrived?"

[AS VICTOR, careful]:
"What about it?"

[AS TESS]:
"It was live thirty-six minutes before the shot."

[NARRATOR]: Silence. Leah's coffee cup stopped halfway to her mouth.

[AS LEAH, processing]:
"Before?"

[AS TESS]:
"Someone knew. The narrative was pre-loaded, ready to deploy the second Geneva went down. This wasn't reactive spin. This was—"

[AS VICTOR, finishing her thought]:
"Premeditated."

[AS TESS]:
"Yeah. But the funding trail dead-ends at shell companies. I need financial forensics I can't do from my laptop."

[NARRATOR]: Ruth stopped pacing. Ahdia could see her calculating—resources, risks, trust.

[AS RUTH, decision made]:
"So we have: professional sniper, translocation tech, and a pre-positioned narrative. This wasn't random violence. This was an operation."

[AS BEN]:
"And I can't trace the translocation without CADENS access logs. Who's authorized to use that tech, when it was deployed, where it pinged—"

[AS RUTH, firm]:
"We're not going to CADENS with this. Not yet."

[AS BEN, frustrated]:
"Then how do we—"

[AS RUTH]:
"We investigate quietly. Until we know more about what we're dealing with."

[NARRATOR]: Ahdia sat on the couch through all of it. Watching. Listening. Her body felt like it belonged to someone else—heavy, distant, wrong. But her brain was doing something it hadn't done in months.

It was paying attention.

Ben needed CADENS access logs. Tess needed financial forensics. They were hitting walls she couldn't climb, couldn't freeze, couldn't punch through with purple lightning.

But Ryu was coming later. For treatment.

And Ryu worked for CADENS.

[AS VICTOR, noticing]:
"Ahdia. You okay?"

[AS AHDIA, flat, distant]:
"Fine. Just tired."

[AS RUTH, concerned but practical]:
"You should rest. Ryu's coming later for treatment."

[AS AHDIA]:
"I know."

[NARRATOR]: She didn't move. Didn't offer to help. Didn't make a TV reference or a dark joke. Just sat there, heavy and wrong, while her brain kept turning over what she'd heard.

Ben needed translocation logs.
Tess needed financial access.
Ryu had both.

## SCENE 2: Ryu Treatment (The Cage and The First Line)

**Touchpoint A:** Ryu arrives for routine treatment, establishes dependency mechanics
**Touchpoint B:** Ryu agrees to look into CADENS intel "off the record"

**Setting:** Penthouse. Later that evening. Ryu arrives for house call. Ahdia on the couch, news playing on mute.

---

[NARRATOR]: The doorbell was the first sound Ahdia had registered in hours. She'd been watching CNN with the sound off—talking heads discussing Geneva's "legacy," which apparently meant arguing about whether she'd been too radical or not radical enough. Nobody on screen seemed to remember she'd been a person twenty-four hours ago.

Ahdia pulled herself off the couch. Every joint ached. The Hyper Seed in her chest felt like a cold stone.

She opened the door.

[AS RYU, professional warmth]:
"Ms. Bacchus."

[AS AHDIA, trying for humor, missing]:
"You can call me Ahdia. You've seen my cellular integrity numbers. We're past formalities."

[NARRATOR]: Ryu smiled—small, careful, like he wasn't sure if smiling was allowed. He carried a medical case that looked like it cost more than Ahdia's first car.

[AS RYU, stepping inside]:
"Fair enough. How are you feeling?"

[AS AHDIA, flat honesty]:
"Like I watched someone's head explode on live TV and then couldn't do anything about it."

[NARRATOR]: Ryu paused. Set down his case. Something in his face shifted—not pity, exactly. Recognition.

[AS RYU]:
"That's... a lot."

[AS AHDIA]:
"It's fine. I'm fine. Everything's fine."

[NARRATOR]: Neither of them believed it. Ryu surveyed the apartment as he set up—TV paused, takeout containers on the counter, evidence of someone who hadn't left these rooms in days.

[AS RYU]:
"Where would you be most comfortable?"

[AS AHDIA, gesturing to couch]:
"This is basically my entire life, so..."

[AS AHDIA, TV framing]:
"Do I sit or lie down? I've only seen this in medical dramas."

[NARRATOR]: Ryu began setting up equipment on her coffee table—portable synthesis device, scanner, vials that glowed faintly in ways Ahdia had learned not to ask about.

[AS RYU]:
"This will take approximately twenty minutes. First scan, then injection, then monitoring."

[AS AHDIA, watching the equipment]:
"That's... a lot more sci-fi than I expected."

[AS RYU]:
"Ruth's breakthrough required adapting isotope delivery systems. Quite elegant, actually."

[NARRATOR]: Ahdia noticed: Ryu respected Ruth's work. That mattered, somehow.

He ran the scanner over her chest. The Hyper Seed registered as a cold spot on the thermal, surrounded by cellular stress markers she couldn't read but knew weren't good.

[AS RYU, clinical]:
"Baseline at 71%. Down from 78% last week."

[AS AHDIA]:
"The riot. The assassination. I burned a lot."

[AS RYU]:
"You burned 50 minutes in three days."

[AS AHDIA, defensive]:
"People were dying."

[AS RYU, careful]:
"I'm not criticizing. Just... noting. Each use costs lifespan. Therefore, each use requires justification."

[AS AHDIA, bristling]:
"I helped investigate an assassination—"

[AS RYU]:
"And burned through 7% cellular integrity for information gathering that conventional analysis could have achieved."

[NARRATOR]: Ouch. Direct hit.

[AS RYU]:
"Your HUD functions in normal time. Thermal overlay, electromagnetic scanning, trajectory mapping—all without temporal manipulation."

[NARRATOR]: Ahdia realized: She'd frozen time unnecessarily. Habit from training, not need.

[AS RYU]:
"Dr. Carter and I can extend your timeline. But you determine how much timeline we have to work with."

[AS AHDIA, processing through TV]:
"Okay, so, this is like... Crank? The Jason Statham movie?"

[AS RYU]:
"I'm not familiar—"

[AS AHDIA]:
"Guy has to keep his adrenaline up or his heart stops. Except mine is 'use powers less or cells stop.'"

[AS RYU]:
"That's... somewhat reductive but not entirely inaccurate."

[AS AHDIA, surprised]:
"Wait, you HAVE seen it?"

[AS RYU, slight smile]:
"I reviewed your media consumption history. Contextual understanding aids communication."

[AS AHDIA, unsure if touched or creeped out]:
"You... studied how I talk?"

[AS RYU]:
"I studied how you think. You process reality through narrative frameworks. It's not ineffective, merely... indirect."

[NARRATOR]: He prepared the injection. She rolled up her sleeve.

[AS RYU]:
"This will feel cold. Then warm. Then nothing."

[NARRATOR]: The needle entered. Cold spreading through her bloodstream—foreign, synthetic. Then warm—body recognizing, accepting, cellular repair activating. Then nothing. But something fundamental had shifted.

[AS AHDIA]:
"So that's keeping me alive?"

[AS RYU]:
"That's extending your cellular viability by approximately 18-24 months."

[AS AHDIA]:
"You're very careful not to call it 'keeping me alive.'"

[AS RYU, something in his tone]:
"Language precision is important in medical contexts."

[AS AHDIA]:
"But I AM dying. Just... slower now."

[NARRATOR]: Ryu paused. Weighed his words.

[AS RYU]:
"Your human cellular structure is degrading from temporal stress. The treatment counteracts this."

[AS AHDIA]:
"That's a very fancy way of saying 'yes, dying.'"

[AS RYU]:
"Degradation and death are not always synonymous."

[NARRATOR]: Ahdia caught it. Weird phrasing.

[AS AHDIA]:
"What does THAT mean?"

[AS RYU, deflecting]:
"It means the treatment is working as designed."

[NARRATOR]: Before she could press, he continued.

[AS RYU]:
"I need to make you aware of something. CADENS monitors temporal distortions globally. When you use your powers, we register the signature."

[AS AHDIA]:
"You're tracking me?"

[AS RYU]:
"We're tracking temporal anomalies. Yours happen to be... distinctive."

[NARRATOR]: Every time she used her powers, CADENS knew.

[AS RYU]:
"This is not surveillance. This is protection. If WE can track temporal signatures..."

[AS AHDIA, finishing the thought]:
"Others can too."

[AS RYU]:
"A flare CADENS can triangulate to within 10 meters. If someone else has that technology, they know where you are and when you're vulnerable."

[NARRATOR]: The weight of it settled. She wasn't just dying faster when she used powers. She was advertising her location.

[AS RYU]:
"This is why we came to you. House call, not facility summons. Protection."

[NARRATOR]: Ahdia understood: The house call was keeping her location private from CADENS staff. A small kindness. Or a leash disguised as kindness.

[AS RYU]:
"You're valuable, Ms. Bacchus. Act accordingly."

[NARRATOR]: He resumed packing. Monitoring complete. But Ahdia's brain wouldn't stop turning.

[AS RYU]:
"Ms. Bacchus, may I ask a personal question?"

[AS AHDIA]:
"That's usually when doctors tell you you're dying faster."

[AS RYU]:
"When did you last leave this apartment for non-essential purposes?"

[NARRATOR]: Silence. Ahdia didn't answer.

[AS RYU]:
"Isolation exacerbates depression. Depression exacerbates risk-assessment impairment."

[AS AHDIA, defensive]:
"I'm not impaired. I did the investigation fine—"

[AS RYU]:
"You burned 7% cellular integrity investigating a crime that had no immediate personal stakes."

[NARRATOR]: Another hit. She'd used powers to feel useful, not because they were necessary.

[AS RYU]:
"The treatment keeps your body functional. But your decision-making determines how long 'functional' lasts."

[NARRATOR]: Depression was making her waste lifespan. The cage was becoming visible.

Freedom costs life. Using powers burns baseline.
Life costs freedom. Treatment keeps her dependent on CADENS.

She can't use powers without being tracked.
She can't survive without treatment.
She can't matter without burning the life she needs treatment to extend.

[AS AHDIA, casual, testing]:
"Can I ask you something?"

[AS RYU]:
"Of course."

[AS AHDIA]:
"CADENS tracks translocation signatures, right? Like, who's authorized, when it's used?"

[NARRATOR]: Ryu's hands stilled on the equipment. Just for a second. Then he resumed packing.

[AS RYU, careful]:
"That's... classified operational data."

[AS AHDIA]:
"I'm not asking for state secrets. Ben found a translocation signature at the sniper nest. He can't trace it without access logs."

[AS RYU]:
"And you want me to...?"

[AS AHDIA, the hook]:
"I don't know. I just want to help. The team's investigating and I'm sitting here getting juice boxes and naps while they do the actual work."

[AS RYU, concerned]:
"Treatment isn't optional. Without it—"

[AS AHDIA]:
"I know. Eleven days. You told me."

[NARRATOR]: The number hung in the air. Eleven days without treatment. That's how long she had before her cells gave up entirely.

[AS AHDIA, quieter now]:
"Geneva was trying to build something. And someone erased her mid-sentence. While I stood there."

[AS RYU]:
"You couldn't have known—"

[AS AHDIA]:
"I have powers that stop time and I couldn't save one person who actually mattered."

[NARRATOR]: Ryu stopped packing. He stood there, medical case half-closed, looking at her with an expression she couldn't name. Concern. Guilt. Something else.

[AS AHDIA, pressing]:
"The team's hitting walls. Ben needs translocation access. Tess needs to trace the bot network funding. And I'm just... here. On this couch. Watching news I can't hear about a woman I couldn't save."

[AS RYU]:
"You're recovering. That's not nothing."

[AS AHDIA]:
"It feels like nothing."

[NARRATOR]: Long pause. Ryu's fingers drummed against the case—three taps, then stop. Ahdia had noticed he did that when he was processing something difficult.

[AS RYU, deciding]:
"I could... look into a few things. Off the record."

[AS AHDIA, looking at him]:
"Really?"

[AS RYU, rationalizing]:
"I'm not promising anything. But if there's information that could help the investigation... you're not wrong that CADENS would have it."

[AS AHDIA]:
"That would be... yeah. That would help."

[AS RYU]:
"I'll see what I can find."

[NARRATOR]: He finished packing. Paused at the door.

[AS RYU]:
"Get some rest, Ahdia."

[NARRATOR]: He left. The door clicked shut.

Ahdia sat alone in her penthouse, treatment coursing through her veins, keeping her human for another few weeks. The cage now visible: dependent on CADENS to survive, trackable whenever she used powers, isolated by depression, wasting the life she needed treatment to extend.

On the muted TV, a newscaster was saying something about a dictator in Eastern Europe. Unrelated. Background noise.

She didn't notice it yet.

But she would.

## SCENE 3: Ruth/Firas Grief Beat

**Touchpoint A:** Ruth alone, late night
**Touchpoint B:** Ruth allows herself to grieve (first time since Firas died)

**Setting:** Penthouse kitchen. Late night. Ruth making tea. Victor enters.

---

[NARRATOR]: Ruth had been standing at the kettle for three minutes. She knew because she'd been counting. Counting was better than thinking. Thinking led to places she couldn't afford to go.

The kitchen was dark except for the blue light of the gas burner. Everyone else had gone home or gone to bed. She'd told them she was fine. She'd told herself she was fine.

The kettle wasn't boiling. She'd forgotten to turn it on.

[AS VICTOR, soft entrance]:
"Can't sleep either?"

[NARRATOR]: Ruth didn't startle. She'd heard him coming—old habit from the ER. Always know who's behind you.

[AS RUTH, not turning]:
"Just thinking."

[AS VICTOR]:
"About Geneva?"

[AS RUTH]:
"About all of it."

[NARRATOR]: Victor moved into the kitchen. Got himself a glass of water. Leaned against the counter like he had nowhere else to be.

[AS VICTOR]:
"You're holding a lot right now."

[AS RUTH, automatic]:
"Someone has to."

[AS VICTOR]:
"That's not what I meant."

[NARRATOR]: Ruth finally turned on the kettle. Watched the blue flame lick the bottom.

[AS RUTH, the name slipping out]:
"Firas would've known what to do. He always—"

[NARRATOR]: She stopped. The name hung in the air like smoke.

[AS RUTH, retreating]:
"Sorry. I don't know why I said that."

[AS VICTOR, patient]:
"Because you're grieving. And you haven't stopped long enough to feel it."

[AS RUTH, defensive]:
"There's no time to—"

[AS VICTOR, firm]:
"Ruth."

[NARRATOR]: She finally looked at him. Victor's face was kind, which made it worse. She could handle cold. She couldn't handle kind.

[AS VICTOR]:
"He told me, you know. About a month before."

[AS RUTH, guarded]:
"Told you what?"

[AS VICTOR]:
"That he was going to propose."

[NARRATOR]: Ruth's breath caught. Her hands stopped moving.

[AS VICTOR]:
"He had a whole plan. After the mission calmed down. He was going to take you to that place you went on your first date—the one with the terrible pasta but the view of the bridge."

[AS RUTH, barely audible]:
"Marcello's."

[AS VICTOR]:
"He said you'd complain about the food the whole time and he'd pretend to be offended and then he'd ask."

[NARRATOR]: Ruth set down the tea. Her hands were shaking. The kettle started to whistle.

[AS RUTH]:
"He never said anything."

[AS VICTOR]:
"He was waiting for the right moment."

[AS RUTH]:
"And then there wasn't one."

[AS VICTOR]:
"No."

[NARRATOR]: The kettle screamed. Ruth turned off the burner. The silence after was worse.

[AS RUTH, cracking]:
"I was going to tell him I loved him. I kept putting it off. Kept thinking there'd be time."

[AS VICTOR]:
"He knew."

[AS RUTH]:
"How could he know if I never—"

[AS VICTOR]:
"Because he knew you. The way you looked at him. The way you always saved him the good coffee. The way you'd check his gear three times before missions."

[NARRATOR]: Ruth was crying now. First time since Firas died. First time since the singularity took him. First time since she'd started holding everything together because someone had to.

[AS VICTOR]:
"He knew, Ruth."

[AS RUTH]:
"I should've said it."

[AS VICTOR]:
"Yeah. You should've. And he should've proposed sooner. And Geneva should've had security that knew what to look for. And none of that changes what's true."

[AS RUTH]:
"What's true?"

[AS VICTOR]:
"That you loved him. That he loved you. That it mattered. And that you're allowed to grieve AND lead. Both things. Not one or the other."

[NARRATOR]: Ruth wiped her eyes. Couldn't find words.

[AS VICTOR]:
"You don't have to be okay right now. You just have to keep going."

[AS RUTH]:
"That's what I'm trying to do."

[AS VICTOR]:
"I know. Just... don't forget you're a person too. Not just the one holding everything together.

[NARRATOR]: He squeezed her shoulder. Left her alone with her tea.

Ruth stood in the dark kitchen. Finally letting herself feel it. The grief. The loss. The proposal that would never happen. The words she'd never said.

Tomorrow she'd be the leader again. Tomorrow she'd hold everything together.

Tonight, she let it fall apart.
