### 8. Thematic Framework Established

**Core Themes Guide Story Decisions:**

1. **You Don't Have to Be Fixed to Be Worthy**
   - Ahdia worthy WHILE struggling in The Between
   - Ruth fights for her AS SHE IS, not "fixed version"

2. **Procrastination Is Not Laziness (Cry for Help)**
   - Book 4: Forced action, can't procrastinate in survival situation

3. **CBT → DBT Journey**
   - Books 1-3: CBT failing (fix thinking, eliminate distress)
   - Book 4: Turning point (accept distress, act despite fear)
   - Books 5-7: DBT succeeding (both/and thinking)

4. **The Sculptor and The Mourner**
   - Ruth: Chooses to open rift (Sculptor) AND risks millions (Mourner)
   - Diana: Chooses transcendence (Sculptor) AND grieves humanity lost (Mourner)
   - Both/and integration

5. **Small Steps Compound**
   - Korede's Chapter 27 choice = result of relationship building over time
   - Not sudden heroism, gradual trust

6. **Asking for Help Is Strength**
   - Ahdia + AR-Ryu partnership
   - Ahdia + Suzie collaboration
   - Ruth's coalition (when works), isolation (when doesn't)

7. **Progress Isn't Linear**
   - Powers gone in Between, return on Earth (non-linear)
   - Setbacks don't erase progress

**Every scene should ask:**
- Is anyone shamed for struggling?
- Is help offered? How?
- What small step is taken?
- What's the dialectic (both/and)?
- What does this tell readers about worth?
