# Book 4 Second Half: Four-Timeline Braided Touchpoints

**Chapters 15-31:** Four timelines converging at F (the rift opening at CADENS HQ)

**Core Theme:** CBT pattern "keep fighting, keep controlling" fails across all timelines

**Time Dilation:**
- Earth timelines (Ruth, Korede, Antagonist): ~2-3 weeks
- Between timeline (Ahdia): Several months subjective

---

## Structure Overview

| Timeline | Characters | Goal | Primary Location | Genre |
|----------|------------|------|------------------|-------|
| **THE BETWEEN** | Ahdia, AR-Ryu, Suzie | Survive, escape | The Between dimension | Survival horror / Romance |
| **RUTH'S QUEST** | Ruth, Tess, Diana, Ryu, Jericho | Wake Mother FAERIS, open rift | Underground CADENS HQ | **Ocean's 11** - Heist |
| **KOREDE'S FRONT** | Korede, Ben, Victor, Leah | Sabotage excavation, defend civilians | Above ground / excavation site | **Dirty Dozen** - War |
| **ANTAGONIST** | Bellatrix, Eidolon, Jericho (secretly), clone army | Excavate Mother FAERIS | CADENS HQ | —  |

### Genre Framework

**Ruth's Quest = Ocean's 11 (Heist)**
- Stealth, deception, infiltration
- Small crew, specific objective (wake Mother FAERIS, open rift)
- Diana CAN'T use powers - Bellatrix would detect any temporal manipulation
- Underground, in the belly of the beast
- Jericho as the inside man (actually the traitor)
- The Score: Wake protocol execution → Rift opens → Ahdia rescued

**Korede's Front = Dirty Dozen (War)**
- Attrition warfare, holding the line
- Defending civilians from clone army assault
- Above ground, fighting toward excavation site
- Objective: Sabotage excavation / buy time
- Ben, Victor, Leah as his squad
- Heavy losses, moral injury, survival against odds

### Why Diana's Powers Are Limited

**Detection Risk:**
- Bellatrix is a more powerful Temporalist
- Any major temporal manipulation lights up like a beacon
- Diana using her powers near CADENS would alert Bellatrix immediately
- Stealth requires she stay "dark" throughout the infiltration
- She's saving herself for the final moment (transcendence cost at Chapter 34-35)

### Convergence Design (Chapter 31)

**The Collision:**
- Ruth's team is underground with Mother FAERIS
- Korede's team breaks through above - sabotage mission reaches excavation point
- They don't know each other is there until they collide
- The rift opens in the middle of the battle
- Chaos: kaiju emerge, clone army engaged, Eidolon commanding
- Ahdia comes through
- Jericho makes his move in the confusion

**Why Separate Until Convergence:**
- Korede refused to help Ruth in Chapter 15 (different priorities)
- Ruth went underground / stealth approach
- Korede stayed above / open warfare approach
- Neither knows the other's exact position
- Converge by accident or necessity at the critical moment

## Chapter Distribution

### Braided Pattern
- **15:** Earth (Ruth - signal detected, coalition splits)
- **16:** Between (Ahdia arrival)
- **17:** Earth (Ruth - Jericho revealed as Bourn's asset; Tess loses FAERIS drone to Mother FAERIS)
- **18:** Between (Ahdia - food discovery, mass grave)
- **19:** Earth (Korede - second wave, Eidolon attack, Leah's stand)
- **20:** Earth (Ruth - analyzing signal, devising picoframe solution, sending one-way broadcast)
- **21:** Between (Ahdia - breakdown after mass grave; AR-Ryu's feelings slip through; bond deepens)
- **22-30:** TBD (message decoded after Suzie meeting)
- **Chapter 31:** Convergence (all timelines)
- **Chapters 32-35:** Aftermath (Diana's transcendence, fallout)

### Dramatic Irony
- Reader sees all four timelines
- Characters only see their own
- Earth: "Every minute counts!" (days of political/military struggle)
- Between: "Have they forgotten me?" (months of waiting)
- Both racing toward same moment from opposite time experiences

## TIMELINE 1: THE BETWEEN (Ahdia)

**POV Character:** Ahdia Bacchus
**Companion:** AR-Ryu (AI), later Suzie Rivets
**Goal:** Survive, escape The Between
**Chapters:** 16, 18, 20, 22, 24, 26, 28, 30 → converges 31

### Touchpoints

| Touch | Chapter(s) | Event | CBT Lesson (Fails) |
|-------|------------|-------|-------------------|
| **C** | 16-18 | Ahdia arrives, meets AR-Ryu, establishes survival base in ruins | Connection in isolation—but can't control being alone |
| **C'** | 21 | Ahdia breaks down after mass grave; AR-Ryu comforts her—not as tool, as Ryu; his feelings slip through; bond deepens | Can't control what you come to feel; can't hide love in isolation |
| **D** | 24-26 | AR-Ryu's battery failing; they plan upload to preserve him; racing toward rift location | Loss beyond recovery looms—can't control entropy |
| **D'** | 28-30 | Alliance with Suzie solidifies; escape plan using Pio's harpoon | Forced to trust stranger with her life |
| **E** | 30 | Suzie sacrifices position to eject Ahdia via harpoon toward rift | Forced to receive help (can't refuse) |
| **F** | 31 | Crosses rift; AR-Ryu dies during ejection (battery); lands on Earth | Bittersweet arrival—saved but grieving |

### Emotional Arc
- **C:** Terror → Survival mode → First relief (AR-Ryu as utility)
- **C':** Breakdown (mass grave) → Ryu comforts her → His feelings slip through → She sees HIM, not the visor → Bond deepens
- **D:** AR-Ryu's battery failing → Dread → Racing against entropy
- **D':** Alliance with Suzie → Combined strength → Plan takes shape
- **E:** Desperation → Forced acceptance of help
- **F:** Escape → Loss (AR-Ryu dies) → Grief in victory

### Key Beats
- AR-Ryu is Ryu's brain copy—not programmed AI. He's been in clinical mode for survival, but he's always been Ryu underneath. His feelings for Ahdia slip through when she breaks down (Ch 21).
- Ryu has always had a crush on her. In The Between, alone, he can't hide it anymore.
- **The Message:** Pio's comm has persistent "interference" - AR-Ryu decodes picoframe video from Earth (Ruth, Ryu, everyone). Message: "If you can see this, return to where you entered. We're reopening the rift but the window is short. Be there."
- Battery countdown creates ticking clock
- Upload plan = hope that AR-Ryu survives → destroyed when battery dies during ejection
- Ahdia kisses real Ryu expecting he remembers → he doesn't → only she carries The Between
- "Tell him I loved her. Tell them about Iteration 3." (AR-Ryu's last words)

## TIMELINE 2: RUTH'S QUEST

**POV Character:** Ruth Carter (primary), Diana, Tess, Ryu
**Key Figure:** Jericho (inside man—secretly antagonist)
**Goal:** Reach Mother FAERIS at CADENS HQ, open rift to rescue Ahdia
**Chapters:** 15, 17, 21, 25, 29 (woven with Korede) → converges 31

### Touchpoints

| Touch | Chapter(s) | Event | CBT Lesson (Fails) |
|-------|------------|-------|-------------------|
| **A'** | 15 | Signal detected (fold echoes/temporal wake from Ahdia's powers); Korede/resistance refuse to help rescue | Can't control allies' priorities |
| **B'** | 17 | Coalition goes rogue; Jericho revealed as Bourn's asset; Tess loses FAERIS drone to Mother FAERIS; Ryu has buried suspicion about Jericho | Control through allies—but who controls whom? |
| **B''** | 20 | Team analyzes signal (fold echoes/temporal wake); Jericho's expertise central; devise picoframe solution; send one-way broadcast to Ahdia | Can't know if message received; faith required; dependent on Jericho |
| **C'** | 19-21 | Journey to CADENS HQ; Jericho provides access/intel | Dependence on unverified ally |
| **D'** | 23 | Setback—security tightens, nearly caught; signal fading | Loss of momentum—can't force timeline |
| **E'** | 25-27 | Korede arrives with forces; combined assault now possible | Forced to receive help (can't refuse) |
| **F** | 29-31 | Reach Mother FAERIS; Jericho calibrates; rift opens | Convergence—success with hidden cost |

### Emotional Arc
- **A':** Hope (signal!) → Frustration (no support)
- **B':** Determination → Relief (Jericho helps) → Unease (too convenient?)
- **C':** Focused mission → Growing dependence on Jericho
- **D':** Despair → Almost give up
- **E':** Shock → Gratitude → Guilt (Korede left civilians)
- **F:** Triumph → Horror (chaos) → Grief (Jericho "dies")

### Key Beats
- Ruth's faith vs certainty: "I don't have proof. I have faith."
- **The Signal:** Fold echoes/temporal wake detected—Ahdia's powers disturbing dimensional space. Ryu, Diana, Jericho all use different terminology (unprecedented phenomenon)
- **Jericho's Expertise:** His "substrate harmonics" interpretation connects to his cosmic radio archaeology—ancient signals he's spent decades decoding. Makes him invaluable (and central to his plan)
- **The Picoframe Solution:** Can't receive from Ahdia, but CAN send. Compress video messages using picoframe tech, pepper the temporal wake. One-way broadcast on faith.
- Diana's quiet knowledge: She knows this rescue may cost her (Geneva debt)
- Jericho's mask: Helpful, supportive, "risking everything"—all calculated
- Tess's FAERIS connection: Can she sense Mother FAERIS's state?
- Ryu trusting his mentor (devastating setup for Book 5 reveal)

### Jericho's Hidden Layer
Every "helpful" act serves his escape:
- Interprets the signal → positions himself as indispensable expert
- Offers intel → positions himself at Mother FAERIS
- Provides access → ensures he's present at rift
- "Calibrates" equipment → sets up cascade failure
- "Dies heroically" → escapes with Mother FAERIS

**Enemy:** Eidolon (embodied), clone avatar army
**Goal:** Defend civilians from Bellatrix's excavation assault
**Chapters:** 17, 19, 21, 23, 25, 27 (woven with Ruth) → converges 31

### Touchpoints

| Touch | Chapter(s) | Event | CBT Lesson (Fails) |
|-------|------------|-------|-------------------|
| **A''** | 15-17 | Clone army + Eidolon assault begins; civilians trapped | Can't control enemy's moves |
| **B''** | 19 | Korede refuses Ruth—must defend civilians HERE and NOW | Can't serve two priorities simultaneously |
| **C''** | 21 | Fighting retreat; heavy losses; moral injury killing clone avatars | Violence has cost even when "necessary" |
| **D''** | 23 | Position deteriorating; evacuation routes closing; overwhelmed | Can't control the tide of war |
| **E''** | 25-27 | Ben tells Korede to GO; Ben's "heroic sacrifice" holding the line | Forced to receive permission (can't refuse Ben's sacrifice) |
| **F** | 29-31 | Arrives at CADENS with forces; kaiju turn tide; pyrrhic victory | Victory through convergence—at terrible cost |

### Emotional Arc
- **A'':** Resolve → Horror (scale of assault)
- **B'':** Guilt (refusing Ruth) → Conviction (right choice)
- **C'':** Moral injury → "They're people in those bodies"
- **D'':** Desperation → Acceptance of likely death
- **E'':** Shock (civilians' choice) → Grief → Purpose
- **F:** Last stand → Unexpected salvation (kaiju) → Hollow victory

### Key Beats
- Clone avatar moral complexity: Non-humans in human bodies—killing them is necessary AND tragic
- Eidolon embodied: Experiencing humanity, concluding it's a disease
- Ben's tactical expertise under pressure
- Victor connecting with civilians, organizing evacuation
- Leah's anger channeled into protection

### Ben's "Death" (E'' Touchpoint)
**The Setup:**
- Position overrun, Korede torn between holding and helping Ruth
- Ben sees Korede paralyzed by duty
- Makes the call: "GO. I'll hold them. Get to Ruth."
- Korede: "I'm not leaving you—"
- Ben: "That's an order. She needs you more than I do."

**The Sacrifice:**
- Ben stays behind with rear guard
- Holds choke point against clone wave
- Last transmission: "Korede, you better make this count. Go get her."
- Then: static
- Everyone assumes he died

**The Truth (Revealed Book 5):**
- Ben survived—barely
- Captured? Escaped through collapse? Found by civilians?
- Returns in Book 5 after being presumed dead
- Suzie's reaction: Just found someone, thought she lost him
- Raises stakes for their relationship

**Why This Works:**
- Cheap but effective (you said it)
- Two "deaths" in Book 4—one fake villain, one fake hero
- Jericho reveal: "He's alive AND evil" (horror)
- Ben reveal: "He's alive AND heroic" (relief/joy)
- Parallel structure, opposite emotional payoffs

### Eidolon's Arc (Within This Timeline)
- First time in human body—feels fear, pain, disgust
- Sees humanity at its worst (war, panic, selfishness)
- Concludes: "They don't need me to fear. They're already afraid of everything."
- No redemption: "Humanity is disease. I'm the cure."
- Retreats at F to build Rogue's Gallery

- She thinks Jericho serves her
- She doesn't know he's been steering events since Book 1

## THE MIRROR STRUCTURE

Three E touchpoints involve forced acceptance of sacrifice:

| Timeline | E Touchpoint | The Sacrifice | The Lesson |
|----------|--------------|---------------|------------|
| **Ahdia** | Suzie ejects her via harpoon | Suzie risks herself so Ahdia can escape | Must receive help you can't repay |
| **Ruth** | Korede arrives (Ben let him go) | Ben "dies" holding the line | Must receive help you didn't earn |
| **Korede** | Ben orders him to leave | Ben sacrifices himself for mission | Must accept others' choices for you |

**Identical CBT Failure:** You cannot control everything—sometimes you must accept help that costs others.

**The Two "Deaths":**
| Character | Appears to Die | Actually | Revealed Book 5 |
|-----------|---------------|----------|-----------------|
| **Jericho** | Falls into rift "heroically" | Escaped with Mother FAERIS | Horror—villain alive |
| **Ben** | Holds line against clone army | Survived somehow | Relief—hero alive |

**Parallel Structure, Opposite Payoffs:**
- Jericho: Mourned as hero → revealed as mastermind villain
- Ben: Mourned as hero → revealed as actually heroic survivor
- Same fake death structure, opposite emotional impact

**DBT Setup:** Characters enter Book 5 having received unearnable grace AND believing they've lost people. The Ben reveal is catharsis; the Jericho reveal is devastation.

### Book 4 CBT Pattern
"Keep fighting, keep controlling what we can"

### Why It Fails (Per Timeline)

| Timeline | What They Try to Control | Why It Fails |
|----------|-------------------------|--------------|
| **Ahdia** | Survival, AR-Ryu's life | Can't control battery death; must accept help |
| **Ruth** | Rescue mission, allies | Can't control others' priorities; must accept Korede's terms |
| **Korede** | Civilian defense | Can't control war's tide; must accept civilians' sacrifice |

### The Lesson Across All Timelines
Control fails. Every attempt to force outcomes creates new problems. The only sustainable path is acceptance + action from values (DBT approach, introduced Book 5).
